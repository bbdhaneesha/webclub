India:culture and customs of India

About

India has always been famous for its traditions.The warmth of people and euphoric celebrations makes our country so special.India culture has a vibrancy of all sorts, the country is an example of tolerance,cooperation,secularism,and social bond among brethren. Indian culture is popular across the world.India culture is considered as the oldest and most diverse cultures of the world.Hindus celebrate diwali,holi and makar sankranti,muslims observe eid,baisakhi is a sikh festival,jains commemorate mahavir jayanti and buddhists mark buddhas birthday. christmas and good friday are celebrated by christians too.Then there are festivals to honour saints,public figures and gurus.


India is a land where people from different religiond coexist harmoniously.79.8% of the population worship hinduism,14.2% islam,2.3% christianity,1.7% sikhism,0.7% buddhism and 0.4%jainism. The cow is a sacred animal in hindu culture and is depicted in mythology as accompanying several gods such as shiva on his bull nandi,or krishna,the cowherd god.


TEMPLES

Exploring India temples is a magical experience,but there are a few things to remember before you visit.you will need to remove your footwear before entering places of worship to prevent any dirt from coming into a cleansed and sanctified environment. men should wear trousers and shirt.women should wear a modest top and trouser.most temples feature one main idol.


FOOD

one of our most favourite customs and traditions in india culture is food! every religion in india has its own distinct cuisine with a signature dish or ingredient. non vegetarian options feature strongly in bengali.india is famous for its delicious fish dishes.gujarat and rajasthan are one of the best states for vegetarian cuisine.


LANGUAGE

There are more than 19,500 mother tongues,415 living languages,and 23 constitutionally recognised official languages in India.hindi is spoken by 41% of the population,particularly in the north,and 12% of indians can speak english as a second language. many people living in india write in the devanagari script,developed between the 1st and 4th centuries.


DANCING

India offers a wide variety of dance forms which vary throughout each state.The hindu sanskrit 'Natyashashtra' recognises eight indian classical dances,which includes kathak in north west and central India,with east India showcasing sattriya from assam,manipuri from manipur and oddisi from odhisa.In south India,you can witness the 'Kuchipudi' in Andhra pradesh, 'Bharatanatyam in tamil nadu',kathakali and mohiniyattam in kerala.
